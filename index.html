Ripple - Explore your world

Project Description

Founders Paul Stavropoulos and Gal Oshri brought me on the team early to help make Ripple easy and fun to use. In a startup environment, it's easy to get lost in the ocean of bug fixes and features to be added. But Paul values UX highly and was determined to stay focused on the user.

I put together a basic user testing strategy that would deliver the quick turn around we needed. I recorded audio of the on-site testing sessions and made sure to observe unbiased, while taking notes. In the following weeks I led whiteboard meetings with two developers and a graphic designer. We were able, through group ideation practices, to craft an intuitive/fun experience. Also, a reliable development priority task list was born.

Today, Ripple continues to grow rapidly. It has been featured by numerous media outlets including CNBC, GeekWire, and Bostinno.

Swap Meet - A video game bartering prototype

Project Description

A part of the Code Fellows curriculum involves project week. Teams are given one week to bring their project from concept to creation. It is a grueling 10 day cycle of code; sleep: eat; code; repeat.

Our Minimal Viable Product was ambitious. Goals included:

  • - allowing two users to create and accept a game trade
  • - a game "favorites" feature
  • - ability to add/edit games via uploader
  • - a fully functional database throughout
  • - a total of 12 layouts

I immediatly led the team communication with multiple versions of user flows. I followed them up with wireframe mockups that allowed Marissa Fellito to begin graphic design. Copies of the wireframes were passed throughout the development process and proved to be the core resource for team communication. For the remainder of the week, I laid out 12 responsive views in Angular.js and built all components from scratch.

I'm proud of my team. Linda Mummy has gone on to work in the back-end of Zillow. Christain Townsden is currently a full stack developer at Expedia. Marissa Felitto is a UI designer for Treehouse in New York City.

MangaJam - A web community redesign

Project Description

MangaJam is a web community dedicated to drawing Anime characters. It is owned by Colin J Young. He hired me via GobySavvy to evaluate his web site's overall user experience. Following the evaluation, Colin hired me to re-design the site based on the issues that I found.

While MangJam had some traditional design issues addressed in the evaluation, I am particulary proud of the wireframes I put together for Colin. I studied at length, the users in this niche community. Understanding the goals/motivations of the user led me to the most important pieces of the puzzle. I consulted with a classical artist of 30 years to understand drawing techniques. I studied the UI that MangaJam users are familiar with (DeviantArt). I used pursuasive user flows to encourage users to share their creations and increase engagement.

Currently, Colin is consulting with a development team in London to bring this re-design to life.
